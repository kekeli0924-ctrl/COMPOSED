Privacy

What we collect, and what we don't.

Composed is pre-launch. This page describes how the app is designed to handle data once the pilot begins. It will be updated before any youth player data is collected.

Who holds the toggles

The player controls what parents and coaches see. Three switches — ratings, coach feedback, development goals — live in the player's settings. The parent dashboard renders only what the player has turned on. The coach's roster sees session compliance only, never private notes.

Video footage

Training clips are recorded on the player's phone. Compression and frame extraction happen on-device before upload — raw video never leaves the phone.

Extracted frames are sent to Google Gemini (via @google/genai) for stat extraction — shots taken, on target, goals, foot usage, pass accuracy. Gemini returns structured counts; Composed stores the counts, not the frames. Raw frames are discarded after analysis.

What gets stored

  • Player identity (first name / handle), position, age band.
  • Session logs (duration, date, counted stats).
  • Coach assignments and roster membership (pilot only).
  • Privacy toggle state — what the player has chosen to share.

What we don't do

  • No third-party tracking or advertising pixels.
  • No selling or licensing of player data.
  • No parent-side analytics that bypass the player's toggles.
  • No retention of raw video after on-device analysis.

Youth data

Composed is built for competitive youth players. In jurisdictions where parental consent is required for minors (e.g. COPPA in the US, GDPR-K in the EU), the pilot will operate under club-administered consent — coaches enroll the team, parents confirm per player before the child's account is activated.

Questions

Email hello@composed.soccer — privacy questions go to the same address as pilot interest and get a human reply.
